A Deep Neural Approach for Paraphrase Detection on Clean and Noisy Text Corpus

Abstract

In the current paper, we propose a deep neural architecture for the task of paraphrase detection. Our approach relies upon determining word and sentence level embeddings, statistically comparing and correlating such embedding pairs, and eventually passing the artifacts thus obtained via a deep neural learner. We experiment with benchmark datasets - a well-established clean text dataset provided by Microsoft and a noisy Twitter text corpus provided in a prior SemEval challenge. Our system performs outperforms the state of the art for noisy Twitter text by a large F1-score of 3.9%.
