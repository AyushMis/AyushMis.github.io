Study and Analysis of Penalty Based Pageranking

Abstract

With the present modernisation and Internet boom there is a flush of information over the internet, thus came the need of finding a robust way to efficiently access data. This has engendered different search engines and algorithms that retrieve information from the internet and rank different web pages based on varied algorithms. Since the main source of revenue for these websites are advertisements more and more websites are increasing the number of ads displayed on them. In order to retrieve relevant information, the search engines should avoid the pages which provide very scarce information while providing a huge number of advertisements. These pages should be treated as penalty pages which should receive a low importance score when they are been indexed. In this paper, we propose a method of ranking web pages which successfully performs the task of giving a very low PageRank score to these penalty pages even if they have a high number of links directed towards or from it (in-links or out-links). The results from the proposed algorithm are been compared with the Googleâ€™s PageRank algorithm which does not consider the existences of penalty pages but ranks the pages just based on the count of the links. It can be clearly observed how well the algorithm performs in achieving the goal of handling penalty pages.
